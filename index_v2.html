📌 About the Study

What is DTAM-MY?

The Digital Telerehabilitation Assessment Model — Malaysia (DTAM-MY) is a PhD-level engineering research project at Universiti Tunku Abdul Rahman (UTAR), led by Chong Yu Zheng (Student ID: 24UED03838).

The study targets elderly post-stroke patients and four supporting stakeholder groups. It aims to produce a reproducible, policy-aligned assessment framework for telerehabilitation technology adoption in Malaysia — validated by GCP-compliant data collection across English, Bahasa Malaysia, Simplified Chinese, and Tamil.

The dissertation is titled: "Development of Digital Healthcare Telerehabilitation Technology Assessments Model and System for Enhancement of Elderly Quality-of-Life."

Research ethics oversight is provided by UTAR UREC under ICH E6(R2) Good Clinical Practice guidelines, the Declaration of Helsinki, and Malaysia's PDPA 2010 (Act 709).

📊 Validated Instruments

Eight Measurement Instruments

All instruments are internationally validated. Scoring and thresholds are preserved precisely as published.

MAST12 items
Kidholm et al., 2012
Model for Assessment of Telemedicine — 7-domain framework evaluating clinical, patient, organisational, and economic aspects of telehealth.
Threshold: S-CVI ≥ 0.80 · Domains ≥ 6/7
TAM7 items
Davis, 1989
Technology Acceptance Model — measures Perceived Usefulness (PU), Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU), and Behavioural Intention (BI). 1–5 Likert.
Constructs: PU · PEOU · BI | α ≥ 0.70
UTAUT210 items
Venkatesh et al., 2012
Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology — 7 constructs: PE, EE, SI, FC, HM, PV, HT. Analysed via PLS-SEM.
AVE ≥ 0.50 · HTMT < 0.85
TUQ10 items
Parmanto et al., 2016
Telehealth Usability Questionnaire — 4 subscales: Usability, System Functionality, RHP Interaction, Privacy/Trust.
Subscale mean ≥ 3.5 · α ≥ 0.70
SUS10 items
Brooke, 1996
System Usability Scale — 5 alternate-reversed items. Score 0–100 computed from raw ratings on 1–5 scale.
≥ 85 Excellent · ≥ 70 Acceptable · < 70 Poor
WHOQOL-BREF26 items
WHO, 1996
World Health Organisation Quality of Life (Brief) — 4 domain scores: Physical, Psychological, Social, Environment. 3 items reversed.
Domain change ≥ 10% at 12 weeks · p < 0.05
ZBI22 items
Zarit et al., 1980
Zarit Burden Interview — measures caregiver burden on 0–4 scale. Total score 0–88 categorised into 4 burden levels.
0–20 Little · 21–40 Mild · 41–60 Moderate · 61–88 Severe
ICF6 items
WHO, 2001
International Classification of Functioning — 4 domains: Body Functions, Activities, Participation, Environmental Factors. Maps rehabilitation outcomes.
≥ 2 urban + ≥ 2 rural site diversity required
